Segmentation Instructions

The first task is breaking up the video into logical segments, so they can be captioned individually. In order to segment most efficiently, hotkeys have been arranged so you can place your hands on the keyboard and not move them while the video plays.

Space Insert a segment break.
Z Remove the next segment break.
X Shorten the current segment (move the next segment break back).
C Extend the current segment (move the next segment break ahead).
Left Go to the previous segment.
Right Go to the next segment.
Up Replay the current segment.

Segmentation Examples

Example 1
Christopher Walken has just finished a sentence, so you press Space to create a segment break. Only, it's Christopher Walken, so it turns out he wasn't _quite_ done.

So you press Left to go to the previous segment (the one you just created), and start tapping C a few times until the segment marker passes the end of the sentence.
Example 2
Abbot and Costello are doing a quick back-and-forth and you press Space to create a segment break. Unfortunately, they were faster than you were, so you caught a bit of the response.

So you press Left to go to the previous segment and tap X to slightly shorten the segment.

Segmentation Interface

As the video plays, a progress bar will show you where you are and where your segment breaks have been placed. The active segment is colored differently.


When progress reaches the end of a segment, the video will pause at the end so that you know exactly where the break is. To continue, go onto the next segment with the Right arrow.

That's it! When the video ends, you'll be offered the choice to do cleanup or move onto the captioning interface.
