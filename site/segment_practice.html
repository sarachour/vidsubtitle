Welcome to Hidden Subtext!
Using the following program, you will be breaking up videos into small chunks by marking where speech starts and ends.
Here are some examples of segments:
To begin breaking up the video, you press the Start button
You may also press the spacebar key instead of pressing the start button.
When you press Start, the video begins to play and the Start button turns into a Break button. You may press Break or spacebar to mark the beginnings and ends of speech.
Try adding at least ten marks to the following video. Use the key combination for Break at least once. Try to mark the start or end of speech.
The segment bar visually displays the segments underneath the video.
The blue markers are where you marked the beginning or end of speech.
The black blocks represent the actual segments.
The grey block is the segment that is currently playing.
The yellow marker is your cursor.
When you click on a segment, the segment is selected and is highlighted in green.
Dragging on a segment shrinks or grows the segment by adjusting the end
right clicking within a segment creates a new marker
There are other controls that you can use to navigate through and edit segments. Hover over each of the controls to read a description of what they do. Invoke each control using both the button and the hotkey.
If you're ever not sure of what a control does or wish to know the keyboard combination, hover over it to see a description.
